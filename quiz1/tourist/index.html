Tourist Attractions in Jember

A simple guide for visitors

Pantai Papuma

Papuma Beach is one of the most popular and well-known beaches in Jember. Although it's approximately 40 kilometers from the city center, the beauty of Malikan's white sand is quite exotic and attracts both local and international tourists.

Kebun Teh Gambir

Jember's Gambir Tea Plantation offers terraced greenery and cool mountain air, the perfect place to escape the hustle and bustle of the city. The paths between the rows of tea plantations are a favorite spot for landscape photography, while the tea-picking process creates an authentic local atmosphere. Come early in the morning for the best light, inhale the aroma of fresh tea, and enjoy a warm cup while overlooking the hills.

Pusat Penelitian Kopi dan Kakao

Imagine walking under the shade of coffee and cocoa trees, listening to researchers tell stories about how Indonesia's finest beans are selected, fermented, and then roasted until fragrant. At the Jember Cocoa Research Center (Puslitkoka Jember), you can witness the complete "farm-to-cup" process, tour the research plantation in Kaliwining, peek into a mini-factory, and then conclude your visit with a taste of Arabica/Robusta coffee and freshly made chocolate. The small museum displays vintage tools and historical archives, perfect for taking photos and learning about Jember's plantation culture.
